We're starting this blog to keep a simple, honest record of how CORA comes together — the decisions, the trade-offs, and the things we get wrong along the way.

## Why start with a waitlist, not a product

Before writing a line of product code, we want to be sure we're solving a real problem for real people. That means talking to freelancers, contractors, and remote employees in Moldova about how they actually get paid, where the friction is, and what a good solution would look like to them.

The waitlist isn't just a signup form — it's how we're validating demand and gathering the first round of feedback that will shape what we build.

## What we're focused on right now

A few things are top of mind in these early weeks:

- Understanding the real cost of receiving foreign income today — in fees, spreads, and time lost.
- Mapping the day-to-day experience of holding EUR/USD and spending in MDL.
- Talking to as many people in our target group as we can, before any design decisions are locked in.

## What to expect from this blog

Updates here will be occasional and to the point — no filler. Expect short notes on what we're learning, milestones as we hit them, and early looks at the product once there's something worth showing.

If you haven't already, you can [join the waitlist](/#waitlist) to get these updates by email as well.
